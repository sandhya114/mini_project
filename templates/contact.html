{% if disease == "Early Blight" %}

Treatment Measures for Early Blight

  • Pyraclostrobin (Headline):Apply pyraclostrobin at a rate of 6 to 12 fluid ounces per acre as a foliar spray, beginning at the first sign of early blight and repeating every 7 to 14 days as needed, ensuring thorough coverage of the foliage.
  • Boscalid:Apply boscalid at a rate of 4 to 7 ounces per acre, starting at the onset of disease or when conditions are favorable for early blight, with subsequent applications every 7 to 14 days to maintain control.
  • Azoxystrobin:Apply azoxystrobin at a rate of 6 to 15.5 fluid ounces per acre as a foliar spray, initiating treatment when early blight is first detected and repeating every 7 to 14 days to ensure continuous protection.
  • Difenoconazole:Apply difenoconazole at a rate of 4 to 8 fluid ounces per acre, beginning at the first sign of early blight or preventatively when conditions favor disease development, with follow-up applications every 7 to 10 days as necessary.
  • Copper-based fungicides:To apply copper-based fungicides effectively, carefully follow label instructions, mix the fungicide with water according to specifications, and evenly spray the solution onto all plant surfaces using calibrated equipment.

Fertilizers

  • Nitrogen (N) fertilizer application ranges from 100 to 150 pounds per acre.
  • Potassium (K) fertilizer application typically ranges from 50 to 100 pounds per acre.

Favorable Conditions for Early Blight

  • High humidity.
  • Low temperature and prolonged leaf wetness.
{% elif disease == "Late Blight" %}

Treatment Measures for Late Blight

  • Acrobat:Apply Acrobat at a rate of 10-14 fluid ounces per acre, ensuring thorough coverage of potato foliage to effectively manage late blight.
  • Use Chlorothalonil at a rate of 1.5-2.5 pounds per acre, ensuring complete coverage of plant surfaces to protect against late blight infection.
  • Apply Mancozeb at a rate of 1.5-3 pounds per acre, ensuring uniform coverage of foliage to provide protection against late blight.
  • If using a product containing EBDC fungicides like Mancozeb, follow label instructions for application rates suitable for late blight control.
  • Apply Dimethomorph at a rate of 4-8 fluid ounces per acre, ensuring even distribution on potato plants to inhibit late blight development.
  • Apply Azoxystrobin at a rate of 6-15.5 fluid ounces per acre, following label instructions for optimal application timing and intervals to manage late blight effectively

Fertilizers

  • Nitrogen (N) fertilizer application ranges from 100 to 150 pounds per acre.
  • Potassium (K) fertilizer application typically ranges from 50 to 100 pounds per acre.
  • Keep phosphorus levels low. While phosphorus is essential, excessive levels can exacerbate disease severity.

Favorable Conditions for Late Blight

  • Warm, rainy, and wet weather conditions.
